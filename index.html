Chapter Overview (8 chapters + toolbox)

In eight concise, practical chapters, you’ll learn the processes and exercises to start or sustain a healthy, ethical, non‑monogamous relationship.

01. Foundations

Build a shared base: map your network, align values, and define terms together so decisions feel clear, expectations match reality, and jealousy has less room to grow.

02. Agreements & Boundaries

Reduce friction with clear, flexible agreements about time, info, health, family, and emotions - plus simple red/amber/green boundaries - so safety and freedom can coexist and renegotiation stays calm.

03. Communication & Check‑ins

Stay aligned with a 30‑minute weekly check‑in, ready‑to‑say scripts, and troubleshooting tips that keep talks grounded, prevent spirals, and make reassurance easy to ask for and give.

04. Jealousy & Emotional Tools

Understand jealousy by naming what it really is, regulate your nervous system, and use empathy scripts to share needs - so hard moments turn into connection instead of conflict.

05. Dates & Practical Logistics

Make dates feel safe and drama‑free: agree on plans before, debrief kindly after, and keep STI practices visible - creating more trust, freedom, and better experiences for everyone.

06. Metamours & Community

Meet metamours without pressure, set respectful boundaries, and build a support network - so you reduce rivalry, avoid triangulation, and make your wider relationship ecosystem sturdier.

07. Breaches & Repair

When agreements break, repair fast with a six‑step process: own impact, offer concrete repair, and prevent repeats - so trust is rebuilt and growth replaces lingering resentment.

08. Maintenance & Growth

Keep love adaptive with quarterly reviews and a simple Done/Keep/Change ritual - celebrate wins, update agreements, and vision forward so you keep evolving together on purpose.

FAQ

Who Is This for?

The Playbook is for couples and individuals who are in — or curious about — ethical non‑monogamy. If you want practical tools you can use tonight (not just theory), you’ll feel at home here. It also helps a visiting partner (“the third”) understand how to fit in respectfully.

  • New to opening up and want a safe, structured start
  • Already open and want to reduce friction, jealousy, and drift
  • Dating someone who is open and want clarity on boundaries and language
How Long Do the Exercises Take?

Most tools take 15–30 minutes. We suggest “micro‑doses”: pick one worksheet this week, try it once, and then adjust together. You don’t need to “finish the whole book” to feel benefits — one small ritual (like a weekly check‑in) can noticeably lower anxiety and increase connection.

  • Quick wins: Weekly Check‑in, Jealousy Trigger, Reassurance Menu
  • Deeper sessions: Agreement Canvas, Quarterly Review
Is This Therapy?

No. It’s a practical toolkit — agendas, worksheets, and scripts — to make hard conversations easier and more kind. Many readers also use therapy; the Playbook gives you shared language and structure between sessions. It does not replace professional care when that’s needed.

Will This Help if My Partner Is Hesitant or Busy?

Yes. You can start solo. Try one 15‑minute worksheet (for example, mapping feelings and needs) and then share a few highlights. The scripts help you make gentle invitations and get to “yes” without pressure. Many couples begin with one person leading; the structure keeps it light and safe.

Is the Playbook Inclusive?

Yes. It’s written to be consent‑first, LGBTQIA+ inclusive, and trauma‑aware. Examples cover different relationship shapes, and metamour guidance focuses on respect, not rules. Use the language that fits you — the tools are adaptable.

How Do We Get Started the First Week?

Keep it simple. Read the short intro, then do one tool together:

  1. Run the Weekly Check‑in (30 minutes) with the provided agenda
  2. Agree on 1–2 boundaries using red/amber/green language
  3. Schedule your next check‑in before you close the session

That’s enough to create momentum and reduce uncertainty right away.
